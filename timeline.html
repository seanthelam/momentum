Staying Organized

üìä Create a Tracking Spreadsheet

Track your applications in a spreadsheet with columns for:

  • Program name and institution
  • Application deadline
  • Submission link/portal
  • Materials required
  • Status (not started, in progress, submitted)
  • Decision date and outcome

üìß Email Organization

Set up email filters and folders:

  • Create a folder for each program
  • Filter confirmation emails automatically
  • Flag important deadlines
  • Keep all correspondence in one place

üìÖ Calendar Reminders

Use your calendar effectively:

  • Add all application deadlines
  • Set reminders 1 week and 2 days before
  • Block out time for writing and editing
  • Schedule check-ins with recommenders

Troubleshooting & Backup Plans

What if I haven't heard back by April?

Don't panic! Programs have different timelines. Some notify in March, others in April or even May. If you haven't heard:

  • Check application portals for updates
  • Continue applying to rolling admission programs
  • Contact programs directly (politely) to ask about timeline
  • Consider local research with CC faculty
  • Look into industry internships or other opportunities

What if I get rejected everywhere?

This is tough but not uncommon‚ÄîREUs are competitive. You have options:

  • Local research: Reach out to CC physics professors about summer positions
  • Industry internships: Many tech companies hire physics students
  • Independent projects: Propose a summer research project to faculty
  • Summer courses: Take advanced physics, math, or gen ed courses
  • Try again: Apply earlier and more broadly next year with stronger materials

What if I can't get letters in time?

Act quickly:

  • Send a polite reminder to your recommenders
  • Offer to meet or provide additional information
  • If necessary, contact programs to explain the delay
  • Some programs may extend deadlines for letters
  • Learn from this‚Äîask even earlier next time!

You've Got This! üí™

The application process can feel overwhelming, but remember: you're taking an important step toward your physics career. Stay organized, start early, and don't be afraid to ask for help from professors, SPS officers, or peers who've been through it. Good luck!
