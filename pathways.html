Physics Major & Minor Pathways

Sample course sequences for completing the physics major or minor at Colorado College. Remember that Block Plan flexibility allows for many variations‚Äîthese are just suggested pathways.

Physics Major (Sample 4 Years)

First Year

  • Physics I: Mechanics
  • Physics II: Electricity & Magnetism
  • Calculus I & II (or equivalent)
  • General Education blocks

Second Year

  • Modern Physics
  • Electronics
  • Classical Mechanics
  • Techniques of Experimental Physics / Observational Astronomy
  • Calculus III
  • General Education blocks

Third Year

  • Electromagnetic Theory and Vector Analysis
  • Thermal Physics
  • Applied Linear Algebra
  • Summer: REU or Research

Fourth Year

  • Senior Capstone
  • Quantum Mechanics I/II
  • Electives (Astrophysics, Solid State, etc.)

Physics Major (3 Years - Accelerated)

For students with AP/IB credit or strong math background

First Year

  • Physics II: E&M (if credit for Mechanics)
  • Modern Physics
  • Calculus III
  • Electronics

Second Year

  • Classical Mechanics
  • Thermal Physics
  • Computational Physics
  • Electromagnetic Theory & Vector Analysis
  • Summer: REU or Research

Third Year

  • Quantum Mechanics I/II
  • Senior Capstone
  • Advanced Electives
  • Consider graduate school applications

Physics Minor

Five physics courses. If a student places out of PC241 or PC242, they must substitute additional physics electives at the PC320 level or higher.

Required Core

  • PC241 - Physics I: Mechanics
  • PC242 - Physics II: Electricity & Magnetism
  • PC251 - Introductory Modern Physics
  • PC261 - Electronics

Choose One More

  • PC320: Topics in Physics
  • PC333: Solid State Physics (not currently offered)
  • PC341: Mechanics
  • PC349: Thermal Physics
  • PC352: Electromagnetic Theory & Vector Analysis (2 credits)
  • PC354: Electromagnetic Waves and Optics
  • PC357: Astrophysics
  • PC358: Extragalactic Astronomy and Cosmology
  • PC361: Techniques of Experimental Physics
  • PC362: Observational Astronomy

Core Course Overview

Introductory Sequence

Physics I - Mechanics: Kinematics, Newton's laws, energy, momentum, rotation, oscillations, and waves. Foundation for all physics.

Physics II - Electricity & Magnetism: Electric fields, circuits, magnetic fields, electromagnetic induction, and Maxwell's equations introduction.

Modern Physics: Special relativity, quantum mechanics introduction, atomic physics, nuclear physics. Gateway to upper-level courses.

Electronics: Analytical theory, simulation, hardware, and circuit design.

Mathematical Foundations

Calculus I-III: Derivatives, Integrals, Differential Equations, Taylor Polynomials, Vectors.

Applied Linear Algebra: Linear equations, matrix algebra, systems, eigenvalues.
