Physics Career Paths

A physics degree opens doors to diverse careers‚Äîfrom fundamental research to technology, finance, education, and beyond. Explore the many paths available to physics graduates.

üéì The Versatility of Physics

Physics majors develop highly transferable skills: problem-solving, analytical thinking, programming, mathematical modeling, and the ability to learn complex systems quickly. These skills are valued across virtually every industry.

  • ~20% of physics bachelor's graduates go directly to physics PhD programs
  • ~30% pursue other graduate degrees (engineering, applied physics, medicine, law, MBA)
  • ~40% enter the workforce directly in STEM fields
  • ~10% enter non-STEM careers or take time before further education

Source: AIP Statistical Research Center

Academic & Research Careers

üî¨ Research Professor / Faculty

Path: Physics PhD ‚Üí Postdoc(s) ‚Üí Faculty Position

Timeline: 10-15 years after bachelor's degree

What You'll Do:

  • Lead a research group conducting fundamental or applied research
  • Teach undergraduate and graduate courses
  • Mentor students and postdocs
  • Write grant proposals and secure funding
  • Publish in peer-reviewed journals
  • Collaborate with researchers worldwide

Pros & Cons:

Pros:
  • Intellectual freedom
  • Work on cutting-edge problems
  • Mentor next generation
  • Job security (tenure)
  • Flexible schedule
Cons:
  • Highly competitive positions
  • Long training period
  • Geographic constraints
  • Lower pay than industry
  • Grant writing pressure

Typical Salary: $70,000-$150,000+ depending on institution and seniority
Note: Sometimes a pro is a con and a con is a pro. This goes for all jobs. It's very dependent on your personality and goals.

üèõÔ∏è National Lab Scientist

Path: Physics PhD ‚Üí Postdoc ‚Üí Staff Scientist at DOE Lab, NIST, etc.

What You'll Do:

  • Conduct research at world-class facilities (accelerators, telescopes, supercomputers)
  • Work on large collaborative experiments
  • Focus purely on research (minimal teaching)
  • Collaborate across institutions and countries

Examples: Fermilab, SLAC, Berkeley Lab, Los Alamos, Argonne, NIST, Naval Research Lab

Typical Salary: $80,000-$150,000+

üéì Postdoctoral Researcher

Path: Physics PhD ‚Üí Postdoc (usually 2-6 years total across 1-2 positions)

A postdoc is temporary research training between PhD and permanent position. Essential for academic careers, common for national labs, optional for industry.

Typical Salary: $50,000-$70,000

Industry & Technology Careers

üíª Data Science / Machine Learning

Path: Bachelor's or Master's ‚Üí Industry

  • Apply statistical analysis and modeling to big data
  • Build predictive models and ML algorithms
  • Work in tech, finance, healthcare, or any data-driven field

Skills Needed: Python, R, SQL, machine learning libraries

Salary: $90,000-$180,000+

üîß Engineering Physicist / R&D

Path: Bachelor's, Master's, or PhD ‚Üí Industry R&D

  • Develop new technologies and products
  • Work on sensors, lasers, optics, semiconductors
  • Common in tech, aerospace, defense, medical devices

Industries: Intel, Apple, Boeing, Lockheed Martin, Medtronic

Salary: $80,000-$150,000+

üíæ Software Engineer

Path: Bachelor's or Master's ‚Üí Tech Industry

  • Design and build software systems
  • Physics background valuable for scientific computing, simulations
  • High demand across all tech companies

Skills Needed: Python, C++, Java, algorithms, data structures

Salary: $100,000-$200,000+ (especially at big tech)

üî¨ Research Scientist (Industry)

Path: PhD ‚Üí Industry Research Lab

  • Conduct applied research in corporate labs
  • More resources, less academic freedom than university
  • Faster path to real-world impact

Examples: Google Research, IBM Research, Microsoft Research, DeepMind

Salary: $120,000-$250,000+

‚öõÔ∏è Quantum Computing

Path: PhD ‚Üí Quantum Tech Startup/Company

  • Develop quantum hardware or algorithms
  • Emerging field with high demand for physics PhDs
  • Work at companies like IBM Quantum, Google Quantum AI, Rigetti

Salary: $120,000-$250,000+

üì° Semiconductor / Hardware

Path: Bachelor's, Master's, or PhD ‚Üí Chip Industry

  • Design and fabricate computer chips
  • Work on next-generation computing technologies
  • Companies like Intel, NVIDIA, AMD, TSMC

Salary: $90,000-$180,000+

Finance & Consulting

üìä Quantitative Analyst (Quant)

Path: Bachelor's, Master's, or PhD ‚Üí Finance

  • Build mathematical models for trading and risk management
  • Develop algorithms for high-frequency trading
  • Physics skills highly valued in quantitative finance

Where: Investment banks, hedge funds, trading firms

Salary: $100,000-$500,000+ (with bonuses)

üíº Management Consultant

Path: Bachelor's or Master's ‚Üí Consulting

  • Advise companies on strategy and operations
  • Analytical skills from physics highly transferable
  • Fast-paced, varied work across industries

Firms: McKinsey, BCG, Bain, Deloitte, Accenture

Salary: $90,000-$180,000+

üè¶ Actuary

Path: Bachelor's ‚Üí Insurance/Finance + Actuarial Exams

  • Assess financial risks using mathematics and statistics
  • Work in insurance, pensions, investments
  • Requires passing actuarial exams

Salary: $70,000-$150,000+

  • Advise government on science and technology policy

  • Bridge science and policy-making

Where: OSTP, NSF, DOE, Congressional offices, AAAS

  • Lucrative career combining science and law

  • Work on satellites, rockets, aircraft, defense systems
  • Apply physics to real-world engineering challenges
  • Companies like SpaceX, Blue Origin, NASA, Lockheed

üéÆ Gaming / Graphics

Path: Bachelor's or Master's ‚Üí Game Industry

  • Develop physics engines and realistic simulations

üå± Entrepreneurship / Startups

Choosing Your Path

ü§î Questions to Ask Yourself

  • Do I love research for its own sake? ‚Üí Consider academic/lab careers
  • Do I want to make a lot of money? ‚Üí Consider tech, finance, or consulting
  • Do I value work-life balance? ‚Üí Consider teaching or industry (avoid academia)
  • Do I want to solve real-world problems? ‚Üí Consider applied research or engineering
  • Am I passionate about teaching? ‚Üí Consider education paths
  • Do I want geographic flexibility? ‚Üí Industry offers more than academia
  • How much more school can I tolerate? ‚Üí PhD takes 5-6 years, consider carefully

üí° Key Insight: You Don't Have to Decide Now

Most careers (except academia) don't require committing in undergrad. You can:

  • Try internships in different fields (research, industry, finance)
  • Work for a few years before graduate school
  • Switch between industry and academia (harder to go from academia ‚Üí industry)
  • Pursue a Master's before committing to a PhD

Summer REUs and internships are great ways to explore career paths!
